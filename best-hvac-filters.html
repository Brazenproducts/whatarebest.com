Understanding MERV Ratings

MERV (Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value) ranges from 1 to 20. Here's what matters for homes:

  • MERV 1-4: Basic fiberglass — protects equipment only
  • MERV 5-8: Captures dust and pollen — minimum for allergy sufferers
  • MERV 9-12: Captures dust mites, mold spores, pet dander — good for most homes
  • MERV 13-16: Captures bacteria, smoke, virus carriers — best for air quality

Higher isn't always better — your HVAC system needs adequate airflow. A MERV 16 filter on a system designed for MERV 8 will strain the blower motor and increase energy costs. For detailed guidance on matching filters to your system, visit homehvacfilters.com.

Frequently Asked Questions

What MERV rating should I use for my home?

For most homes, MERV 11–13 offers the best balance of filtration and airflow. MERV 13 captures bacteria and smoke particles. Go higher only if your HVAC system can handle the reduced airflow — check your manual or ask your HVAC tech.

How often should I change my HVAC filter?

Standard 1-inch filters should be changed every 30–90 days. 4-inch filters last 6–12 months. If you have pets, allergies, or live in a dusty area, change more frequently. A dirty filter makes your system work harder and costs you money.

Are expensive HVAC filters worth it?

Yes, if you value air quality. Premium filters like BowTie Filters capture more particles and last longer, reducing the total cost per month. Cheap fiberglass filters protect your equipment but do almost nothing for air quality.
