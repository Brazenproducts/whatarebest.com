Best Mattresses (2026)

Updated April 2026 · Compared innerspring, memory foam, hybrid, and all-foam mattresses for every sleep style

Mattress shopping is one of those experiences where the internet has made things simultaneously better and worse. Better because you can actually compare things without driving to twelve stores. Worse because every review site seems to either be a thinly veiled affiliate funnel or a collection of vague impressions that do not help you actually decide anything.

The truth is most people are sleeping on the wrong mattress, and the wrong mattress is usually not dramatically wrong, just slightly wrong in a way that erodes sleep quality over years. A good mattress for your body type, sleep position, and temperature preferences is one of the best investments in actual quality of life you can make.

These are the mattresses worth serious consideration, starting with the one I would point most people toward first.

$$$ · Luxury innerspring hybrid · Best overall sleep quality for most people

Saatva built a reputation by doing the basics better than most of the competition. The Classic is a two-sided innerspring hybrid that you can actually flip, with targeted lumbar support, multiple firmness options, and a luxury feel that a lot of bed-in-a-box competitors still cannot match. It is more expensive than a foam mattress in a box, but it also sleeps noticeably better in most cases.

If you want a traditional luxury mattress experience without the traditional luxury mattress shopping nightmare, Saatva is the answer. They white-glove deliver and set it up in your room, which sounds trivial until you remember what moving an actual mattress into a third-floor bedroom actually involves.

  • Real luxury sleep quality, not just marketing

  • Multiple firmness options at purchase
  • Good edge support and traditional innerspring feel
  • More expensive than most mattress-in-a-box options
  • Heavier and harder to move
  • Not ideal for people who prefer memory foam hug

Runner Up

Purple Hybrid or Purple Plus

$$$ · Gel grid technology · Best for hot sleepers and people who hate memory foam

Purple built its reputation on a grid gel top that is genuinely different from standard foam. If you overheat at night, hate the sinking feeling of memory foam, or want something that feels more responsive and less huggy, Purple is worth serious consideration. The grid design also handles pressure points differently than layered foam, which a lot of people find more comfortable for hip and shoulder sleeping.

It is not the right mattress for everyone. Some people find the grid surface takes getting used to, and the feel is definitely different. But if you have tried memory foam and been underwhelmed or overheated, Purple is usually the next logical destination.

  • Gel grid is genuinely different and often better for hot sleepers
  • Responsive without the memory foam sink
  • Good pressure relief for side sleepers
  • Unique feel that a lot of people prefer once they try it
  • More expensive
  • The grid feel is not for everyone
  • Edge support could be better on some models

Best Value

Nectar Memory Foam Mattress

$$ · Memory foam value leader · Great pressure relief at a reasonable price

Nectar became popular by offering memory foam quality closer to the Tempur-Pedic experience at a dramatically lower price. The mattress ships in a box, has a longer trial period than most competitors, and consistently earns strong marks for motion isolation, pressure relief, and overall value. If you want memory foam without Tempur-Pedic pricing, Nectar is the smart entry point.

The tradeoff is that memory foam mattress quality varies more than most brands admit. Nectar is the one that tends to land in the solid-to-strong range consistently, which makes it easier to recommend blindly than a lot of its competitors.

  • Strong memory foam quality at a reasonable price
  • Good motion isolation for couples
  • Long trial period and decent warranty
  • Easy to buy and set up with bed-in-a-box delivery
  • Memory foam feel is not universally loved
  • Can sleep hot on some models
  • Heavy to move once set up

How to Choose the Right Mattress

Sleep position and body type matter more than you think

Side sleepers generally need more pressure relief at the hips and shoulders. Back sleepers need decent lumbar support without being too firm. Stomach sleepers usually need something firmer to avoid sinking in ways that strain the lower back. This is not optional context. Buying a mattress without thinking about your actual sleep position is how people end up with the wrong thing.

Firmness is subjective and inconsistent across brands

One company's "medium firm" is another company's "firm." Do not rely on the marketing language. Look for the specific layers, materials, and thickness specs instead. That is where the real information lives.

Temperature is a bigger factor than most buyers realize

Memory foam sleeps hot for a lot of people. If you already overheat at night, the added body warmth from slow-recovery foam can turn a decent mattress into a sweaty experience. Hybrid and gel-grid designs tend to sleep cooler. This is worth checking before you buy rather than discovering it six months in.

The Bottom Line

Saatva makes the best overall mattress for most people who want genuine luxury sleep quality, while Purple is the better answer for hot sleepers and people who dislike traditional memory foam. If you want strong value, Nectar is the memory foam pick. If the budget is tight, Zinus is the realistic entry point. Just remember that sleep quality compounds over years, and a slightly more expensive right mattress beats a cheaper wrong one.
