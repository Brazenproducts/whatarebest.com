MERV Ratings Explained (Simply)

MERV = Minimum Efficiency Reporting Value. Higher number = catches smaller particles. Here's what matters:

  • MERV 8: Dust, pollen, lint. The minimum you should use.
  • MERV 11-12: + pet dander, mold spores, fine dust. Best for most homes.
  • MERV 13: + bacteria, smoke, virus carriers. Best for allergies/asthma.
  • MERV 16+: Hospital grade. Your home HVAC probably can't handle the airflow restriction.

Don't go above MERV 13 for residential. Higher ratings restrict airflow too much for standard HVAC systems, which makes your system work harder, costs more to run, and can cause premature failure.

How Often to Change Your Filter

The "every 30 days" advice on cheap filters is because cheap filters clog fast. Quality MERV 11-13 pleated filters genuinely last 90 days in most homes. If you have multiple pets or live in a dusty area, check monthly and replace when visibly dirty.

The Bottom Line

MERV 12 is the sweet spot for most homes — Nordic Pure if you want value, Filtrete 1900 if you want the best. Skip anything below MERV 8, and don't go above MERV 13 unless your HVAC tech specifically says your system can handle it.
