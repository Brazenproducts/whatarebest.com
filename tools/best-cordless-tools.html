Best Cordless Tools (2026)

Updated April 2026 · Compared Milwaukee M18, DeWalt 20V, Makita 18V, and Ryobi One+ for home use and job site capability

Buying cordless tools is one of those decisions that feels like it should be simple and then becomes surprisingly complicated once you start comparing voltage systems, brushless motor claims, cross-platform battery compatibility, and the fact that every brand swears their tools are the best. The honest answer is that the major brands all make good tools. The real decision is ecosystem and use case.

If you are a homeowner who needs a drill, impact driver, circular saw, and maybe a string trimmer a few times a year, Ryobi One+ is probably the smartest buy because the battery platform is affordable and covers almost everything a homeowner needs. If you are a serious DIYer or tradie who needs tools that perform at a professional level every day, Milwaukee M18, DeWalt 20V, or Makita 18V are the serious answers.

These are the cordless tool ecosystems worth your attention, starting with the one I think most buyers should start with.

How to Choose the Right Cordless Tool Ecosystem

The battery platform is the real purchase

Buying a tool is just the beginning. The battery platform is the long-term investment. Every tool you buy in that ecosystem should share batteries and chargers. That is why picking the right ecosystem matters more than picking the right individual tool. Committing to Milwaukee M18, DeWalt 20V Max, or Ryobi One+ means your future purchases are all built around that battery system.

Homeowner versus professional changes the math

For homeowners, Ryobi One+ makes the most financial sense because the tools are affordable and cover everything you are likely to need. For professionals or serious DIYers, Milwaukee, DeWalt, or Makita make more sense because the durability and performance pay for themselves over years of hard use.

Brushless motors are worth the premium

Every major brand now offers brushless versions of their key tools at a price premium over brushed models. That premium is worth paying. Brushless motors are more efficient, run cooler, last longer, and deliver more power in a smaller package. If you are buying anything beyond the most basic tool, go brushless.

The Bottom Line

Milwaukee M18 is the best cordless tool ecosystem for professionals, because the performance, durability, and battery system set the standard. DeWalt 20V and Makita 18V are equally legitimate professional options that come down to brand preference and specific tool availability. For homeowners, Ryobi One+ is the smart buy because the ecosystem covers almost everything you need at prices that make sense. Just buy once and commit to the platform.
